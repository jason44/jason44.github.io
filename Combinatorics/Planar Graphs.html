Planar Graphs

A graph is planar if it can be drawn in a plane without edges crossing. A plane graph refers to a planar depiction of a planar graph.

Planar graphs have a lot of applications from circuit design to street maps.

Showing a graph is planar

Circle-chord method

Find a circuit that goes through every vertex in the graph (they don't always exist, but this method is for when it does). This group of edges is called the circle. The remaining edges are called chords. A chord must obviously be drawn either inside or outside the circle in a planar drawing(plane graph). Choose the first chord. If properly chosen, it will force another chord to be either inside or outside, and so on.
Essentially, the decision of the first chord forces the outcome of the rest. If a point is reached where a chord must cross another chord, regardless of whether it is inside or outside, by contradiction the graph must be nonplanar (because chords cannot intersect in a plane graph representation of a planar graph).

It can be shown that any nonplanar graph always contains a (a complete bipartite graph of consisting of two sets of vertices) or a (complete graph on five vertices) as a subgraph.
