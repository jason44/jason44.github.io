A graph is connected if every pair of vertices can be joined by a path.

Otherwise, the graph is disconnected
We say that a vertex or edge disconnects a graph if the resulting graph is no longer connected after the edge or vertex is removed.

A matching graph is a graph where each vertex has one edge incident to it. That is, two edges share no common vertices. Matching can be best described as pairing off adjacent vertices.

A complete graph is a graph where every pair of distinct vertices is connected by a unique edge.
