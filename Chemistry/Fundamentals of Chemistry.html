Substances and Mixtures

If the properties of a mixture vary throughout the sample, it is heterogenous. If the properties of the mixture does not vary throughout the sample, then the mixture is homogenous.

Conservation of Matter and Energy

Chemistry rests on these two well-established physical principles. In a chemical reaction, matter and energy is both conserved.

  1. Matter is neither created nor destroyed, only rearranged from one substance to another
  2. The energy stored in molecules of two different substances are intrinsically different, and chemical energy may be converted into thermal, electrical, or mechanical energy during reactions.
    These two core principles must be slightly modified in nuclear reactions because they occur at energies so high that matter and energy can be converted to one another through Einstein's relation . The sum of mass and energy is still conserved in nuclear reactions.

Law of Definite Proportions

In 1794, Joseph Proust published the fundamental law of definite proportions which says:
In a given chemical compound, the proportions by mass of the elements that compose it are fixed, independent of the origin of the compound or its mode of preparation.
For example, any amount of sodium chloride always contains chlorine by mass, whether this sodium chloride is obtained from salt mines, crystallized from ocean water, or synthesized from elements.

John Dalton's Atomic Theory

John Dalton proposed he following postulates which form his atomic theory of matter

  1. Matter consists of indivisible atoms.
  2. All atoms of a given chemical element are identical in mass and in all other properties
  3. Different chemical elements have different kinds of atoms; in particular, their atoms have different mass.
  4. Atoms are indestructible and retain their identities in chemical reactions.
  5. A compound is made up of its elements through the combination of their respective atoms in small whole-number ratios.
    1. That is, the ratio of atoms of unlike elements in a chemical compound is consistent.
