Orientation

Orientation of a vector space is the arbitrary choice of what bases are positively oriented and which one is negatively oriented.

In Euclidean space, the two possible orientations are right-handed and left-handed.

Right-handed spaces have the property that counter-clockwise rotations are positive (consistent with our idea of angle) and clockwise rotations are negative.
Left-handed spaces have the property that clockwise rotations are positive.

By convention we say that a right-hand orientation is positive and a left-hand orientation is negative.
