Basic Differential Equation Models

Classifying Differential Equations

A differential equation can be expressed in terms of the highest order derivative
or a form in which all of the dependent terms are in one side
A differential equation is linear if it can be expressed as a linear combination of the dependent terms

The order of a differential equation is the highest order derivative in said equation.
eg: If the highest order derivative in an equation is a second order derivative, then the differential equation is a second order differential equation.

Mathematical Models

When modeling observations in physics, ecology, etc. some useful tips for constructing models with differential equations:

  1. Identify the dependent and independent variables. Often the independent variable is time.
  2. Choose appropriate units
  3. Understand basic principles of the field, ie in physics, use Newton's laws of motion when constructing the model.

Sometimes a model may be a system of differential equations.
